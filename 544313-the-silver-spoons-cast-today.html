1. Ricky Schroder

Ricky Schroder played Rick Stratton in the show. He began his acting journey at the tender age of 9, playing the son of Jon Voight in the 1979 movie The Champ. The young actor won a Golden Globe Award for Best New Male Star, making him the youngest-ever award winner.

His role in Silver Spoons pushed him into the limelight as a child star. However, after the sitcom ended, he found it difficult to escape being typecast as the blonde, youthful heartthrob.

Schroder became known as Rick and started taking on more mature roles in the late '80s. He starred in the Western miniseries Lonesome Dove in 1989 and later became part of the ensemble cast of intense TV shows like NYPD Blue and 24.

He has also directed three films, including Black Cloud, featuring Tim McGraw, and the Hallmark movie Our Wild Hearts. In addition, he acted in TV films such as Dolly Parton's Christmas of Many Colors: Circle of Love and Dolly Parton's Coat of Many Colors.

2. Joel Higgins

Joel Higgins acted as Edward Stratton III. He began his career as a special services sergeant, handling entertainment for the U.S. Army. The TV personality spent several years performing in musicals, including the first national tour of Grease.

He then became a regular on the soap opera Search for Tomorrow. After cancelling his cowboy comedy series Best of the West in 1982, he joined the Silver Spoons cast, where he became famous for portraying the ultimate man-child.

Once Silver Spoons concluded, Higgins starred in a comedy about priests called Have Faith, which unfortunately didn't resonate with audiences and only lasted for seven episodes. Higgins has acted in a few TV movies and episodes in the 80s and 90s, with his last acting role being alongside Nathan Lane and Gabriel Byrne in the 2016 comedy No Pay.

6. Corky Pigeon

As a child actor, Corky Pigeon portrayed Freddy Lippincottleman, a friend of Ricky, in the initial four seasons of Silver Spoons. But what happened to this young star after the show?

In 1987, two years after his last appearance on the show, Pigeon decided to step away from TV and movies. Now, Pigeon identifies himself as a dedicated Christian, a loving husband, a father, and a businessman.

He is recognized for his role as the lawyer Leonard Rollins in the first season of the TV sitcom show. As of 2024, he is still alive at 77, although he hasn't taken on any acting roles since the late 1990s.

9. Franklyn Seales

Franklyn Seales joined the cast of Silver Spoons in its second season, taking on the role of Edward's new business manager, Dexter Roosevelt Stuffins, after acclaimed actor Leonard Lightfoot left the show. He also appeared in episodes of Hill Street Blues and Amen.

After Silver Spoons, Seales' final significant achievement was his performance in Nothing Sacred, an adaptation of Ivan Turgenev's novel Fathers and Sons, at the Mark Taper Forum in October 1988. Besides acting, Seales was also a painter.

Unfortunately, Seales passed away in 1990 at the age of 37 due to body complications due to AIDS.

Why was Silver Spoons cancelled?

NBC first cancelled Silver Spoons in 1986 after four seasons (along with Punky Brewster) because the network saw that both shows couldn't compete as strongly as they hoped against 60 Minutes on CBS. The show was later picked up for another season in first-run syndication, ending in May 1987 after 116 episodes.
