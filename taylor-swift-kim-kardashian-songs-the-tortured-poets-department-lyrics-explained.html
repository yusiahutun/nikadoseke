Taylor Swift Revisits Kim Kardashian Feud on &quot;The Tortured Poets Department&quot;

April 14, 2024
blog

It doesn’t look like Taylor Swift is burying the hatchet with Kim Kardashian any time soon.

The singer seems to have revived her old rivalry with the reality star with “thanK you aIMee” and “Cassandra,” two bonus tracks included on The Tortured Poets Department: The Anthology, a surprise edition of the brand-new album (which came out today) featuring a full 15 additional songs, which dropped at 2:00 a.m. The first of the two even appears to spell out Kardashian’s first name in the title, by way of its unconventional capitalization.

The feud between the two A-listers allegedly began in 2016, after Kardashian’s then-husband, Kanye West, released the track “Famous,” which open with explicit lyrics about Swift that he claimed she had approved beforehand: “I feel like me and Taylor might still have sex / Why? I made that bitch famous.”

When Swift publicly denied approving the lines, Kardashian released an edited three-minute video recording of an alleged phone call between West and Swift, in which the singer appeared to consent to West using the controversial lyric. As Swifties will recall, the backlash against her was immediate and brutal, leading Swift to retreat from the public eye and later release her vengeful sixth studio album, Reputation. (When the full recording of the 25-minute call was leaked in 2020, it did confirm that West had not included the word bitch when sharing the lyrics with Swift.)

In “thanK you aIMee,” the singer compares Kardashian to “a bronze spray-tanned statue” and a school bully who “threatens to push me down the stairs.” She also accuses the reality TV mogul of publicly ridiculing her in the aftermath of the 2016 scandal by “[writing] headlines” and “laughing at each baby step I’d take.”

Despite the uphill battle, Swift notes that the adversity made her stronger: “I wrote a thousand songs that you find uncool / I built a legacy which you can’t undo / But when I count the scars, there’s a moment of truth / That there wouldn't be this, if there hadn’t been you.” She also admits to hiding Kardashian’s real identity in a song: “And so I changed your name, and any real defining clues / And one day, your kid comes home singin’ / A song that only us two is gonna know is about you.”

A similar theme of betrayal and defamation plays out in “Cassandra,” with Swift describing her own exoneration “when the truth comes out.” The song’s namesake is most likely the Cassandra of ancient Greek mythology, who was blessed with the power to foresee disaster, but cursed to never have her prophecies believed. Swift compares herself to the Trojan priestess, singing, “They set my life in flames, I regret to say / Do you believe me now?” She also calls out the hypocrisy of a family that touts Christian values while simultaneously pursuing greed: “The family, the pure greed, the Christian chorus line / They all said nothing / Blood’s thick but nothing like a payroll / Bet they never spared a prayer for my soul / You can mark my words that I said it first / In a morning warning, no one heard.”

Besides resurrecting the feud with Kardashian, both songs set the scene for the highly anticipated release of Reputation (Taylor’s Version), the rerecorded version of the 2017 album. And “Cassandra” in particular mentions snakes, which are prominently associated with Reputation.
