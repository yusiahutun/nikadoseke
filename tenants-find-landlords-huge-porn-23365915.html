Tenants find huge porn mag collection after hearing 'thumping' sounds in creepy attic

March 10, 2024
blog

A huge porn magazine collection was discovered by two tenants after they climbed into their attic to investigate a strange noise.

Daniel Sims and Ash Day were overjoyed when they found the huge stash of vintage European titles - with one referring to the mags as a "gold mine".

The pair, from Huddersfield, West Yorkshire, were hanging out at their rented home when they suddenly heard a strange noise coming from the loft.

Concerned that someone was breaking into the house, they decided to investigate and climbed into the attic for the first time since they'd moved in a year earlier.

They didn't come across any intruders, but instead found a mountain of porn kept in pristine condition and wrapped in cellophane.

The duo were amazed and instantly brought some of the secret stash down to flick through it - and believe it belongs to their landlord.

"The estate agent had said there was nothing in there and it's tricky to access," Daniel, 32, told Jam Press.

"We had to climb up the bathroom door to get in and, once we were in, found the room empty, except for right at the back we found stacks of porn."

Daniel regularly explores abandoned properties and shares these on his YouTube channel Bearded Reality, so decided to film the whole thing.

In the video, he can be seen precariously balancing in the dusty attic, having accessed it from the bathroom.

He said: "We decided to investigate what the noises would be in the creepy attic of death.

"We just got up here and, not even kidding, we found a stash of porn - wrapped-up porn."

Daniel and Ash, 25, then flick through a few of the magazines, finding various types of pornography, all over 20 years old with some printed in Italian and Spanish.

The front cover of one adult magazine, which appears to be an old edition Fiesta, reads 'Is yours as meaty as this?' in a large font, with a topless woman bent over.

Other titles include Mayfair and Men's World.

Daniel can then be heard asking his friend if he knows who the previous residents were, as Ash replies that he believes it was a group of students.

But the pair quickly realise students are unlikely to own "age old porn", and that it most likely belongs to their landlord, though they haven't asked.

"This is incredible, what a find," Ash says as he looks through the collection.

The video later shows the bathroom floor littered with adult magazines.

"I didn't know there was that much porn in the world," Ash can be heard saying.

After climbing out of the attic, Daniel films an update, saying: "After safely getting down from the attic of hell - which turned out to be heaven - and, as you can see, it's a different mood entirely.

"It's the craziest amount of porn that you can find - look at the stack of it."

The dirty magazines can be seen stacked up in a towering heap while Ash adds he has been "sitting on a f***ing gold mine".

The huge pile then topples over, spilling over the kitchen floor and hitting the bin, leaving the duo in hysterics.

Daniel adds: "It broke the bin, that's how heavy this porn stash is."

He later uploaded the duo's findings to his YouTube channel, where one fan called it "the pre-internet motherload".

"It was clearly a looked after collection because of how it was wrapped up to keep it in good condition," Daniel told Jam Press.

"It was very random having loads of vintage porn but nothing else up there.

"We assume it belongs to the landlord or a previous resident."

This isn't the first time Daniel has stumbled upon erotica.

Just a month ago, in December 2020, he found another collection while exploring an infamous building dubbed 'Dildo Manor'.

Sadly, the dilapidated property didn't live up to its name - as there were no sex toys - however, the explorer did uncover stacks upon stacks of BDSM porn magazines.

Daniel has been documenting his adventures after starting his YouTube channel in 2019.

He is currently planning to explore the site of the 1986 Chernobyl nuclear disaster in Ukraine.
