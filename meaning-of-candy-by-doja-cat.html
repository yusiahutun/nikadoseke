Meaning of Candy by Doja Cat

September 11, 2024
blog

The song "Candy" by Doja Cat explores the enticing yet deceptive nature of relationships and the allure of superficial sweetness. The overall theme of the song is centered around the idea that appearances can be deceiving, and the pursuit of something sweet and pleasurable can often lead to unfulfillment and disappointment.One standout lyric that contributes to this theme is "She's just like candy, she's so sweet, but you know that it ain't real cherry." Here, the comparison between the woman and candy represents the surface-level attraction and temporary pleasure that the relationship provides. The term "real cherry" suggests that what appears sweet might not always be genuine or authentic. This lyric can be seen as cautionary, reminding listeners that not everything that appears delightful is actually good for them.Another important lyric that reinforces the theme is "I can be your sugar when you're fiendin' for that sweet spot, put me in your mouth, baby, and eat it 'til your teeth rot." This metaphorical description of being someone's sugar represents the intoxicating allure of satisfying one's cravings or desires. However, the mention of teeth rotting alludes to the negative consequences that can arise from indulging in temporary pleasures without considering the long-term effects. This lyric suggests that pursuing instant gratification can lead to consequences that can ultimately erode an individual's well-being.Furthermore, the lyrics "And it's such a typical thing, running over billion men who only deal with difficult things" emphasize the common nature of seeking out superficial relationships to avoid dealing with deeper emotional issues. It implies that both parties involved are using each other as distractions rather than engaging in genuine connections. This theme sheds light on the tendency to prioritize immediate satisfaction over the potential for deep and meaningful relationships.In the bridge of the song, the lyrics "Sugar coated, lies unfolded, you still lick the wrapper. It's addictive, you know this, but you still lick the wrapper" suggest a self-awareness of the deceptive nature of the relationship. The sugar-coated lies symbolize the appealing facade that masks the truth within. Despite knowing that the relationship is based on falsehoods, the individuals involved continue to cling to the addictive cycle. This highlights the addictive nature of superficial relationships, even when the participants are aware of the lack of substance.Overall, "Candy" intelligently crafts a metaphorical narrative around the appealing yet deceitful nature of relationships. The song's lyrics explore themes of superficial attraction, short-lived pleasures, avoidance of emotional depth, and the addictive allure of instant gratification. Doja Cat cleverly invites listeners to reflect on the nature of their own relationships and the importance of seeking authenticity and substance beyond the initial allure.
