Leicester's s***house celebration leaves Birmingham fans 'violated in their own stand'

May 10, 2024
blog

Leicester City midfielder Stephy Mavididi marked his second goal against Birmingham City with one of the bolder celebrations you will see this season.

The 'Wagatha Christie derby', as it was coined by supporters in tribute to the famous libel battle between Wayne Rooney's wife Coleen and Jamie Vardy's spouse Rebekah, saw the Foxes overcome City 3-2 in the Championship. It is a match which will be remembered for a large number of cheeky Leicester fans wearing paper masks emblazoned with Vardy's face.

However, those who did stop looking at the amusing away contingent would have seen Mavididi, 25, celebrate an impressive brace with one of the cheekiest celebrations of the season. It was so bold, football fans were left aghast.

The former Juventus star stabbed home his second to give Enzo Maresca's side a 3-1 lead at St. Andrews. After watching the ball bobble into the back of the net, he nonchalantly climbed over the advertising barrier, sat on top of it, folded his arms and stared at a whole stand of furious Birmingham fans.

Many of them strode down the steps and gesticulated in his face, before his team-mates joined in with his joy. "How’s he got away with that celebration? Incredible s***housery," one fan commented.
