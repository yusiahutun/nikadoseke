Where to buy the Celine Triomphe

June 10, 2024
blog

Sometimes in fashion, things come full circle. Or half-circle – in the case of Celine’s Triomphe bag. The accessory may only be a few years old but its crowning glory, the double-C ‘Triomphe’ motif, dates back to the 1970s – an emblem of the French house’s rich heritage.

The Triomphe bag as we know it was born not in Paris, but in Los Angeles. There, Celine’s then-new creative director Hedi Slimane dreamt up his inaugural collection for the maison – including a bag that would quickly become a house icon.

It was 2018 and the French-born designer was studying Celine’s archives, his interest piqued by the 1970s and in particular, an insignia designed by Céline Vipiana, the house’s namesake and founder. The motif, formed by two inverted Cs, was originally conceived not by accident but as a result of one – a minor collision on place de l’Etoile, to be precise. Vipiana stepped out of the car and noticed that the chain encircling the Arc de Triomphe was made up of a mirrored C – or half-circle – motif. As a tribute to Vipiana, Slimane named both the crest, and the new bag bearing it, the ‘Triomphe’.

A triumph it was. Since its debut at Celine’s spring/summer 2019 show (Slimane’s first for the house) the Triomphe bag has been seen on the arms of some very stylish women, including Karlie Kloss, Margaret Qualley, Alexa Chung, Brie Larson and Kaia Gerber (who owns the style in at least three colourways). Angelina Jolie also has multiple versions, which she styles with daytime sundresses and dressed-up, plissé maxis alike – a testament to the Triomphe bag’s versatility.

With its gently rounded, rectangular shape, gold hardware and perfect proportions – all rendered in Celine’s signature, not-too-shiny, not-too-matte, ‘satin’ calf leather – the Triomphe feels enduringly modern, with a subtle, Seventies sensibility. And if it wasn’t already practically perfect in every way, its shoulder strap is extendable, allowing it to be worn over the shoulder or across the body. It does it all – and has enough room for all the day-to-day essentials.

Celine

Slimane has expanded Celine’s Triomphe bag collection to include larger and smaller iterations of the original Classique style, in signature Triomphe canvas or calfskin in classic black and natural, as well as seasonal shades such as lychee, lavender and hot-pink. For evening, there are chain-laden options, from the curved Besace to the architectural Trapeze, reimagined in foiled metallics and sequins, or with shimmering, crystal-embellished closures. There is even a super-luxe, made-to-order version of the bag; the Haute Maroquinerie Triomphe comes gilded with 18-karat gold hardware and takes a single artisan 12 hours to make.

Whatever the finish, every bag that bears the Triomphe name is crowned by the maison’s double-C clasp – a symbol of the history of this particular hero, and an emblem that’s as chic now as it was in the 1970s. Trust us, the Triomphe’s got staying power.
