Who is Kevin Hines?

Kevin Hines gained fame after attempting to jump off the Golden Gate Bridge on September 25, 2000. He is a multi-award-winning filmmaker, best-selling author, and an award-winning global self-murder prevention and mental health advocate.

His jump at the Golden Gate Bridge makes him part of the 36 people who have survived the 220-foot jump.

Kevin Hines was born on August 30, 1981, in San Francisco, California, United States of America. He will be 43 years old in 2024.

Hines’ childhood wasn’t that glamorous. According to his motivational talks, he lived in squalor on the streets of San Francisco, California, and his parents struggled with addiction. His parents' addiction problem resulted in them often leaving Kevin unattended in motels and with no adult care or supervision.

The situation of their parents got Kevin into Child Protective Services. However, attempts to get Kevin and his brother into the same household failed, resulting in a life out of foster care.

How did Kevin Hines survive?

After his jump and landing in the water, a lifeline from the most unexpected places appeared. A sea lion positioned itself below Kevin Hines’ body, keeping him afloat until the Coast Guard arrived to move him to safety.

Of course, the incident struck Kevin Hines as he did not expect to survive the jump. He considered it an answer to his prayer from God while falling. Kevin also saw this as another opportunity in life, a chance to heal, find hope, and recover.

Kevin Hines' injuries

Was Kevin Hines injured during the fall? During the fall, Kevin Hines’ body rotated, and he hit the water while sitting. He took the impact on his legs and up through his back.

Three of Kevin’s vertebrae were shattered from the fall, lacerating his lower internal organs. Kevin was rescued by the US Coast Guard and transferred to the hospital in San Francisco for treatment, where he underwent emergency surgery. He made a full recovery.

Does Golden Gate Bridge have a net?

The Golden Gate Bridge has a Safety Net. Hines, his father and other people affected by self-murder at the bridge advocated for a solution for two decades, which resulted in its construction.

Construction of the protective area started in 2018 and was finally completed in 2024. The net is 20 feet below the sidewalk and extends 20 feet from the bridge.
