UK theme park giving FREE entry to anyone called Archie following royal baby birth

August 01, 2024
blog

Drayton Manor Theme Park marked the birth of Meghan Markle and Prince Harry’s newborn son, Archie Harrison Mountbatten-Windsor, with its own special announcement.

The Staffordshire theme park said anyone called Archie would be able to walk in free of charge.

All they will need to do in order to bag the freebie is give their name at the gate and bring a form of photo ID.

And it’s not just the lucky Archies who could end up bagging some savings.

Thrill seekers accompanying Archies could see ticket prices slashed, with adult fares (12+) dropping from £39 t0 £19.50.

Children aged four to 11 and seniors will also net savings with the standard price of £29 being reduced to £14.50 for those who turn up with an Archie in tow.

Drayton Manor is a popular family destination with rollercoasters, zoo animals and Thomas Land – for fans of the cheeky train and his pals.

The theme park is also the home of the Apocalypse ride – the world’s first stand-up drop tower ride.

William Bryan, the park’s managing director, said: “We’re delighted to welcome Archies to the park this weekend to celebrate the birth of the beautiful baby boy.

“It’s always exciting to welcome a new royal baby into the world, and we wish Meghan and Harry the best of luck – hopefully, we’ll be seeing them in Thomas Land next year for Archie’s first birthday.”

Meanwhile, Harry, 34, and Meghan, 37, will be spending precious time with their first child.

The pair showed off their little bundle of joy who will have no royal title, just being known as Master Archie.

He is seventh in line to the throne and made his world debut at Windsor Castle on Wednesday.
