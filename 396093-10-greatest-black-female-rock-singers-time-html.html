10 greatest black female rock singers of all time

February 07, 2024
blog

Black women have made significant contributions to the music industry for many years. Many of them started composing songs in different styles as early as the 1870s. As the years passed by, they remained influential and helped to popularise the R&B genre. But one genre many people seem to have forgotten is rock. Over the years, black female rock singers have performed and recorded some of the best rock and roll music and even helped to overturn the idea that rock is a form of male rebellion.

The thing with black female rock singers is they do not have the large following compared to that of their counterparts in R&B and other genres. But many of them have the artistic freedom to imagine, create, and distribute influential rock music.

The efforts of black rock musicians led to the popularisation of the Afropunk movement. They have also managed to curate an environment that many people describe as an ethereal and momentary hideaway for black people.

10 greatest black female rock singers that you need to know

There have been many black female singers, either solo or in rock bands, over the years. However, a few stand out from the rest. These are the greatest black female rock singers of all time.

1. Brittany Howard

Before Brittany Howard became one of the greatest black women in rock, she used to be a commercial sanitation worker and even worked as a mail delivery woman. But while working in different environments, she played covers and original songs with the blues-rock band, Alabama Shakes.

2. Donna Summer

In the late 1960s, Donna Summer, one of the greatest black female rock stars of all time, joined the blues-rock band called Crow. Although it dissolved, she went on to release her debut studio album titled Lady of the Night in 1974. The songs in the album consisted of the pop/rock/folk variety.

In 1978, she became the first female artist in the modern rock era to have the number one single on the Hot 100. For her contributions in rock, she won a Grammy Award in the Best Female Rock Vocal Performance. In 2013, she became an inductee of the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

3. Etta James

Etta James had a powerful, deep, and earthy voice that made it easy for her to sing songs in the rock and roll genre. She first ventured into rock genre in 1973 and released a self-titled album that got a Grammy Award nomination. However, she kept changing her musical style throughout her career.

As a result, many overlooked her contributions to music. But when she started to bridge the gap between rhythm and blues and rock and roll, she won many awards and became one of the greatest black rock n roll singers of all time. Etta James is an inductee of the Rockabilly Hall of Fame and Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

4. Grace Jones

Undoubtedly, Grace Jones is one of the most astounding visual acts in pop music. For many years, she embarked on singing a variety of musical styles, including R&B, disco, new wave, art pop, and reggae. Her unique style has made it challenging for many people to follow in her footsteps.

But despite her versatility in many music genres, she was brilliant whenever she sang a rock song. Grace Jones created groundbreaking rock music back in the 1980s. In 1999, VHI ranked her at number 82 in the list of 100 Greatest Women of Rock and Roll.

5. Joan Armatrading

Joan Armatrading released her debut album in 1972, which blended jazz and folk. She had a style of music that drew an array of influences from other genres, including reggae, soul, blues, jazz, folk, and rock. Her career took her from 70s folk-pop to 80s rock.

Some of her greatest rock songs include My Myself I and Drop the Pilot. In 1980, she received a Grammy Award nomination for Best Female Rock Vocal Performance. She also received the same nomination in 1983 for her work in her eighth studio album titled The Key.

6. Nona Hendryx

Nona Hendryx launched a solo career in 1977 and released a self-titled album that blended soul and rock. Rock was not a popular genre among the black community when she was at the peak of her career. At that time, most black female artists were doing pop and disco songs.

As a result, people described her as rebellious. Although her first album garnered favourable reviews, female rock soloists were not getting enough airplay. Being black made it even more challenging. Nona did what she loved and went on to become one of the greatest women in rock history.

7. Poly Styrene

Poly Styrene was a British musician, singer, and songwriter. Throughout her career, people identified her as the frontwoman for the punk rock band X-Ray-Spex. It was one of the few rock bands with female singers formed in 1976. Poly Styrene had a stirring voice and eclectic songwriting skills that enabled her to shine in a white male-dominated genre of punk rock.

8. Skin (Deborah Anne Dwyer)

Deborah Anne Dyer, known by the stage name Skin, is a songwriter, electronic music DJ, and singer. She is the lead vocalist of the rock band Skunk Anansie, once considered to be one of the most successful UK chart acts of between 1952 and 2003. Skin has played an instrumental role in popularising the group due to her powerful, wide-ranging soprano voice.

9. Tina Turner

Tina Turner has had a successful music career that spanned over 60 years. In that period, fans described her as the Queen of Rock and Roll. She sang with swagger, sensuality, and unstoppable energy. Like many other black women in music, she crossed racial obstacles and overcame fierce oppression to inspire rock as a music genre for everyone.

Tina Turner was a giant in the industry. She won over 160 awards, received over 180 nominations, and inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame, Rock & Roll Hall of Fame, and the St. Louis Walk of Fame. At 81, the queen of rock and roll holds the record with four awards given for Best Female Rock Vocal Performance.

Between 1988 and 1997, she held the record for the largest paying rock concert attendance for a solo artist. Undoubtedly, Tina Turner is simply the best and one of the greatest black rock and roll artists of all time.

10. Tracy Chapman

When Tracy Chapman released her debut album in 1988, politics dominated the scene and everything revolved around politicians. At that time, a young black woman with an acoustic guitar singing rock music would have seemed irrelevant. But that was not the case with Tracy Chapman.

She shifted the balance of musical power, and when she released her debut single, Fast Car, in 1988, the male fraternity that dominated the rock music genre quickly noticed her as a worthy competitor. Chapman rose to fame swiftly and deadly. For her efforts, she won four Grammy Awards, including Best Rock Song and two Best Female Rock Vocal Performance awards.
