50 Cent is not engaged to longtime girlfriend Cuban Link despite huge rock on her ring finger

July 31, 2024
blog

50 cent and longtime girlfriend Cuban Link do not have plans to walk down the aisle any time soon.

TMZ reported that the 47-year-old producer - who is developing a TV series with Eminem - and the fitness trainer are not engaged, despite her wearing a huge diamond on her ring finger recently.

A representative for the rapper told the publication 'the ring is not an engagement ring, but the couple is very much together.' 

Engagement rumors were sparked when the New Jersey-bred beauty uploaded an Instagram reel that showed her supporting the music artist's performance from backstage. 

Cuban - born Jamira Haines - was noticeably wearing a gigantic sparkler on her ring finger in the clip.

Cuban soundtracked the snippet with Summer Walker's 2019 song Session 22.

And in the caption to her 1.8 million followers, she used white heart and padlock emojis. 

TMZ noted that Haines has worn the ring as far back as last summer, though fans didn't seem to notice then. 

The couple began dating in August 2019 and in July 2020 the two were rumored to have broken up, but they have steadily been going strong.

The twosome tend to stay mum on their relationship, which they debuted at the season 6 premiere of 50 Cent's - real name Curtis Jackson - TV series Power.

The Candy Shop hitmaker dished to People that his girlfriend is 'cool' and added that she 'has her own ideas and her own direction.'

As for his professional life, earlier this year 50 Cent confirmed that he and Eminem, 50, are developing a series based on the critically acclaimed film 8 Mile.

After rumors that the pair were 'reportedly' working on the series together surfaced online, the father-of-two took to Instagram to confirm the news.

He also appeared in two seasons of For Life, a crime drama that ran on ABC beginning in 2020.

8 Mile was a semi-autobiographical story about Eminem's own rise to fame. He starred in the film as Jimmy Smith Jr, a car factory worker struggling to gain respect on the underground rap circuit.

The film won a number of awards, most notably Best Original Song for Lose Yourself at the Oscars in 2003.

8 Mile co-starred Kim Basinger, Michael Shannon and Anthony Mackie, and rappers Xzibit, Proof and Obie Trice had small roles in the project.
