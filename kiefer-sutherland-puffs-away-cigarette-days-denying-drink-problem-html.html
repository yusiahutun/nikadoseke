Just days ago he denied having a drink problem despite being dragged into a hotel after a night of revelry.

But that did not stop Kiefer Sutherland indulging in another vice by puffing away on a cigarette as he went for a walk in New York on Monday.

The 24 star looked like he was having a great time as he got a hit of nicotine while strolling around the Big Apple.

A 24 a day man? Kiefer Sutherland looked like he was enjoying his cigarette in New York on Monday

While the 47-year-old looked quite presentable in grey pullover trousers and leather shoes, he hardly looked like a star about to appear on The Tonight Show later in the day.

It remains to be seen whether the Canadian star will talk about fan fears for his health during his chat with the programme's fawning host Jimmy Fallon.

While walking through an airport car park in Los Angeles last week, a photographer kept asking the son of Donald Sutherland about his past alcohol issues and specifically his wild night in London

Although the actor didn't acknowledge his antics in England, when asked if 'fans should be worried,' Kiefer emphatically responded 'no.'

While he was hardly at his rugged best as he sauntered around town, it was still a far cry from how the star looked following the night out where he partied at the now almost unbearably trendy Chiltern Firehouse a fortnight ago.

Vice city: He seemed to be enjoying getting a nicotine hit while sauntering around the Big Apple

Are you Lost Boy? Despite his appearance was going to be appearing The To Tonight Show later

He required assistance from two burly minders when he finally arrived back at his hotel, and even  had to be dragged up the steps outside.

Kiefer would have needed more than 24 hours to recover from his night out at the venue, where he partied alongside such luminaries as Poppy Delevingne, Danielle Lineker and Metallica axeman Kirk Hammett.

It is not the first time his fun-loving personality has got him into trouble however, as back in 2007 he pleaded guilty to his fourth DUI charge

Later he pleaded no contest to the DUI charge and was sentenced to 48 days in jail.
