Beauty is pain - as Kim Kardashian and Julianne Moore know all too well. Celebrities love their high heels, and, earlier this week, both stars were caught wearing towering heels that looked incredibly painful.

Yesterday, Kim Kardashian was spotted in Los Angeles tottering around in a pair of perspex stilettos.

The socialite and reality TV star, who is seven months pregnant, was out in Beverly Hills running errands with mother Kris - a difficult task for the 32-year-old, considering how swollen her ankles looked. 

Kim Kardashian (left) and Julianne Moore (right), perhaps wishing they were more footloose and fancy free

The one time you wouldn't want to be in their shoes: Kim Kardashian's feet (left) look painfully swollen as she stepped out in Beverly Hills yesterday, while Julianne Moore's toes (right) jut out at angles as she walks the red carpet at Cannes this week

Indeed, it is a wonder Kim was even able to fit in the shoes given how swollen her ankles were and how tightly the shoes clearly pinched.

A day earlier, Julianne Moore had attended the Cannes Film Festival Opening Ceremony in a stunning lilac Dior dress.

The actress, 52, had nothing but smiles for the camera - but a closer look at her feet told different story.

Both little toes were poking out on either side from what looked like very uncomfortable silver heels.

Kim and Julianne aren't the only ones to fall foul of the foot faux pas though. The unofficial red carpet dress code demands high heels, and actresses and models usually conform.

Actress Emmy Rossum went to the Season two launch of TV series Shameless wearing a silver sleeveless dress by Ralph Lauren, which she matched with thin, strappy sandals.

Top model Kate Moss went to a 2012 Marie Curie fundraiser (with husband Jamie Hince) dressed to kill. At least, her feet - which looked red and cramped in her shoes - might have felt that way. Her toes look pinched and cramped.

Emmy Rossum (left) at the 2012 launch of Season two of Shameless and Kate Moss (right), pictured with her husband Jamie Hince, at the 2012 Marie Curie fundraiser

Ouch! Emmy Rossum's pinky toe peeks out from her tight heels, while Kate Moss's digits look cramped

Charlize Theron went to the 21st Annual Gotham Independent Film Awards in New York wearing a daring white top, black skirt, and heels with thin gold straps.

Shoe-holic Sarah Jessica Parker attended a ballet gala premiere wearing a Stella McCartney white lace dress which she matched with green suede stilettos.

Both actresses' feet look uncomfortable, with SJP's looking particularly veiny.

From afar, Charlize Theron (left) and Sarah Jessica Parker (right) look great on the red carpet

Close up, though... Maybe a girl CAN have enough shoes?

Actress Emma Stone and Samantha Cameron, wife of Prime Minister David Cameron, both opted for stilettos for recent public appearances. The shoes look great, but are they worth the pain?

Close-ups of their feet reveal Emma has blisters on the back of her ankles while Samantha's feet bulge from the tight shoes. Considering she was on a charity walk, she might have hung her heels up and put on a pair of trainers.

Emma Stone (left) at the 2012 premiere of 'The Amazing Spiderman' and Samantha Cameron (right) at the Born to Walk Tall charity walk in 2011

Emma's blisteringly high heels have left her with sores on the back of her ankles

Walking? In those heels? A pair of flats might have been more sensible - SamCam's toes look cramped

Christina Hendricks and Meg Ryan, spotted out and about dressed down, wore flip flops that seemingly revealed the damage shoes can wreak.

Christina's flip flops revealed reddened feet with dramatic bunions, while Meg's looked to have rubbed to the point that she has been forced to bandage her feet with plasters.

You certainly wouldn't want to be in their shoes, would you?

Laid bare: Wearing flip flops, Christina Hendricks (left) and Meg Ryan (right) reveal the damage done to their feet

Gives a whole new meaning to down-at-heel, doesn't it?
