New dating site to launch on Valentine's Day for 'desperate' singles

April 12, 2024
blog

Many singletons may be dreading Valentine's Day this month.

After all, it's never ideal when people rub their love lives in your face.

But, to make things a little better, a new dating site is launching on the same day to soften the blow.

Bluntly named Desperate.Dating, it targets people who are "desperate".

The app encourages people to "have fun and embrace your desperation" – and it's looking for candidates who have been on the market for three or more years.

It's set to launch on Valentine’s Day, –and nearly 3,000 people have already snapped up the chance to join.

At the moment around 200 people are joining the site each week.

According to Metro, businessman David Minns, who set up the site, said: "I hope we will appeal to people who are fed up with the usual advice from relationship experts such as 'don’t text too often' and 'don’t commit too soon'.

"As far as we are concerned the more desperate you are the better.

"Hopefully we can come to their rescue."

The graphics include statements such as "desperate to know you" and "desperate for you to text" – and the aim of the game is to ensure people wear their hearts on their sleeves.

It's said, up until now, people from Sheffield have been single for the longest, as they've been flying solo for the average time of seven years.

Meanwhile people from Wakefield have been on the market for six years, and those looking for love from Edinburgh have been flying solo for five years and ten months.

But it's important to remember there's absolutely nothing wrong with being single.

Don't let the numbers worry you, as sometimes we all need a little "me time".

What some people call "desperate" others could call "I'm very much happy on my own".
