KILEA registration for learners: Requirements and procedure

March 31, 2024
blog

Kenya made a major education overhaul – replacing the 38-year-old 8-4-4 system with a new 2-6-3-3-3 system that aims to fit society's needs better. The new education system has been accompanied by a transformative change in the testing of learners. Now, in its second year, the Kenya Intermediate Level Education Assessment (KILEA) will take centre stage. However, most people are still unfamiliar with KILEA – including its requirements and registration process.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

The registration of learners wishing to sit for the 2024 Kenya Intermediate Level Education Assessment (KILEA) is ongoing. It commenced on January 29th 2024, and ends on March 29th 2024.

What is the meaning of KILEA?

The Kenya Intermediate Level Education Assessment (KILEA) developed by KNEC is administered to learners in the intermediate level who are ready to transition to the pre-vocational level.

KILEA learners registration

Want to register for KILEA exams? The Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC) has introduced the KILEA portal, an online platform designed to streamline the registration process for stage-based learners.

All stage-based learners must have valid assessment numbers to be registered from the KILEA portal. Those without assessment numbers are registered through the normal registration process in the CBA portal under the intermediate portal. Here are the steps on how to register learners through the KILEA portal:

  • Go to the CBA portal and log in using valid credentials, which include username and password.
  • Upon successfully logging into the CBA portal, click the "KILEA portal" button. (The KILEA portal homepage shall be generated to register KILEA learners).
  • Click the "Register Learner of KILEA" button to generate the registration page.
  • Enter the assessment number and click on the "Search" button. (Learner details shall appear for verification and updating to register).
  • To edit or add missing learner details, click on the specific field to key in or select details where applicable.
  • You must select one or more disability options.

Required registration learner details

During registration in the KILEA portal, all details of current learners can be edited except for the assessment number. Schools must guarantee the accuracy of learners' registration details. The editable registration details include the following:

  • Full name
  • Gender
  • Date of birth
  • NEMIS UPI code
  • Birth certificate number
  • Religious subject (CRE/IRE)
  • Parent/Guardian name
  • Parent/Guardian phone number
  • Disability option (physical disability/deaf/low vision/blind/none)
  • Nationality

All necessary learner registration details are mandatory, except for the birth certificate number and NEMIS UPI code, which should be provided if accessible. Moreover, for stage-based learners, it is imperative to select one or more options for disability.

What is the difference between KPSEA and KILEA?

The Kenya Primary School Education Assessment (KPSEA) is administered to grade 6 learners ready to transition to junior secondary school.

Who sits for KILEA exams? KILEA is administered to learners at the intermediate level who are ready to transition to the pre-vocational level. Learners who go to the pre-vocational level are those with intellectual disabilities, severe cerebral palsy, autism, blindness, the deaf and multiple disabilities.

The pre-vocational curriculum is predominately skill-based. It prepares learners for pre-requisite and basic work skills in various vocational areas to help them identify their interests and abilities in a given field.

FAQs about KNEC exams (KILEA and KPSEA)

  • What grade is the KPSEA for? Under KPSEA, 60% of the total score is from the school-based assessment. This means the examinations are independently conducted in the school at the end of grades 4,5 and 6.
  • How many marks are there in the KPSEA exam? The KPSEA exam has a maximum weight of 40 marks. This is added to other assessments that the learners did in grades 3, 4 and 5, each marked out of 20. When combined, it adds a total of 60 marks.
  • What is KILEA in full? KILEA stands for Kenya Intermediate Level Education Assessment.
  • What is exceeding expectations in KPSEA? Performance level 4 is the highest academic commendation a grade 6 pupil can get and is described as exceeding expectations.
  • What happens after grade 9? At the end of grade 9, learners will sit for an assessment to determine the readiness for the senior school. At this stage, learners begin to specialise based on their career choices.
  • What happens when you fail the KNEC exams? According to the KNEC policy, you can re-sit failed or referred examination paper(s)/ subject(s) for a maximum of five (5) times within five years after the first sitting.
