Chris Lighty's estranged wife 'was NOT fighting with her husband before he killed himself'

The former wife of hip-hop mogul Chris Lighty has publicly disputed the idea that a fight with her had pushed the 44-year-old man to commit suicide on Thursday, instead saying that his financial problems were likely to blame.

‘He was in a lot of pain and he possibly had some financial difficulties,’ said Veronica Lighty's friend Norman Downes, speaking on her behalf as she was too distraught to make a statement.

The medical examiner ruled on Friday that Lighty, the long-time manager of Mariah Carey, 50 Cent, Cee Lo Green and P Diddy, died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound to the head at his home in The Bronx, New York, Thursday morning.

Tragic death: Chris Lighty with his wife Veronica, at Kanye West's 30th birthday five years ago; he shot himself after the pair argued on August 30

Lighty’s suicide came amidst a bitter divorce from his 36-year-old wife. However, Downes insisted that the estranged couple did not fight that day, according to the New York Daily News.

The 44-year-old entertainment heavyweight left his home in South Riverdale on Thursday with the words, ‘I’m tired of this,’ walked to the backyard and shot himself in the head with a 9-mm. handgun, sources said.

Veronica Lighty, speaking through Downes, suggested that it was not a row with her that led to her ex-husband’s suicide, but rather his massive IRS debt totaling $5million. The Associated Press, however, reported that Lighty had paid most of it off, owing only $330,000 in state and federal taxes.

When asked if financial problems might have provoked Lighty to commit suicide, Downes said, 'The only person that really knows is Chris, and he ain’t here.'

Grieving: Fans and friends of the mogul gathered in the street where he was found dead in Riverdale

Support: Lighty's friends hug one another as medical examiners work at the scene

Lighty’s business partner Michael ‘Blue’ Williams has offered a different take on what may have pushed the successful music manager over the edge.

‘Chris' debt is probably less important than the pressure that he was under,’ Williams told MTV News. ‘What people are missing and what people don't see and what Chris is an example of is how much, as black men, we carry around and don't always communicate.’

Williams said for big-time entertainment managers like Lighty, getting in debt is part of the business, and major money woes can be easily remedied ‘when your artist gets hot.’

‘I know exactly what he was feeling when it comes to pressure and debt, trying to keep your company afloat and doing what you got to do, while also maintaining a certain perception out there,’ Williams explained.

Lighty, founder of Violator Management, merged with another talent company to form Primary Violator with Williams around the time of his divorce.

Musicians flocked to twitter to pay tribute.

Ice T wrote: 'Wow! > RT @clighty: Rest in peace to my main man The Grand Incredible Dj Scott La Rock. I am still Criminal Minded. 8/27/87'

'R.I.P. Chris Lighty,' posted Fat Joe on his Twitter account. 'That man saved my life.'

Russell Simmons wrote on Twitter: 'RIP Chris Lighty. Today, we lost a hip-hop hero and one of its greatest architects.'

Hip hop architect: Lighty with Sean 'Diddy' Combs in 2008

Justin Bieber’s manager Scooter Braun wrote: 'hard to believe it is true but RIP to my friend Chris Lighty. gone too soon.'

Rihanna tweeted: 'Rest peacefully Chris Lighty, my prayers go out to family and loved ones! Dear God please have mercy (sic)'

Lighty was a leading figure in the music world from 1988 when he joined Russell Simmons and now-Warner chief Lyor Cohen at Rush Management.

Famous friends: Tributes poured in on Twitter for the hip-hop bigwig

He said in a statement: 'I am deeply saddened by the loss of a hip-hop hero.

'Chris Lighty has been a dear friend of mine since he was a kid. Was a brilliant partner in business and I was so proud of all that he had accomplished.

'He is an amazing example of how a passionate kid from the street can go to the most even-keeled, smart, thoughtful manager in the business and a generous philanthropist. He was loved by everyone who knew him, including me. He will be missed greatly by all of us.'

Grieving: Lighty is Mariah Carey's longterm manager; tributes poured in from stars including Rihanna, right

Chris has worked with artists including Busta Rhymes, Q-Tip, Missy Elliott, Fantasia, Mya, Diggy Simmons, Ja Rule, Mobb Deep, LL Cool J and Nas.

Page Six reported in November 2011 that he was hit hard by financial issues and divorce from his wife of seven years Veronica.

An NYPD spokesman told Page Six: 'At 11.33 hours police responded to a person shot in the rear of 670 W 232nd Street. When we arrived we found a male, 44, with a gunshot wound to his head.

A firearm was recovered nearby. He was declared dead at the scene. It is being investigated as a possible suicide.'
