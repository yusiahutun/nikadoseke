CS Nakhumicha Says Govt Is Working on Resolving Doctors' Strike in Next Few Days

May 22, 2024
blog
  • Health Cabinet Secretary (CS) Susan Nakhuimicha has given the country a ray of hope, as the government has expressed its willingness to have talks with the doctors
  • The CS assured Kenyans that the situation is under control and an agreement could be reached in a few days to come
  • She further expressed her confidence in implementation of the Social Act to resolve the issues raised by the striking doctors'

Health Cabinet Secretary (CS) Susan Nakhumicha has said the government is in the process of ending Kenya Medical Practitioners Pharmacists Dentists' Union's (KMPDU) strike.

According to Nakhumicha, the government has taken time to resolve the doctors' strike because they were working on finding an impactful long-lasting solution that will not witness frequent strikes.

''I want to assure Kenyans that as government, we are working at the clock to an amicably and lasting solution is reached. What we don't want as government is to put a badge on a wound without treating it, that is why we are taking our time,'' Nakhumicha stated.

Nakhumicha calls for talks to end KMPDU's strike

While speaking in Kajiado County on Tuesday, Nakhumicha further revealed of meeting that have yielded a green light to the situation.

She said the situation was under consideration, and an agreement could be reached in a few days.

''I have had three meetings and I want to assure Kenyans that this situation is going to be put in control if not tomorrow, in the next few days we are going to reach an agreement,'' she revealed.

The CS further disclosed that the government had extended to have talks with the striking doctors and present their offer and urged the doctors to join the negotiations.

''I want to ask our doctors that we serve common patient. I want to let them know that the government has given them an offer, it is up to them to come to the table, and we discuss their counter offer, we are willing to talk,'' she disclosed.

The Social Health Act

In countering the effects of limited resources, Nakhumicha expressed confidence the Social Insurance Health Fund (SHIF) Act will collect resources and resolve the issues raised by the doctors.

She said the country was in the current situation because of limited resources accessed have been not been optimised fully.

'The crisis that we are in today is because resources are not infinite, they have to be looked for and once we get resources we have to optimise them. That's why the Social Act is very important. It will ensure that we collect resources, buy medicines and equipment for our hospital and pay the doctors,'' she said.

Calls for CS Nakhumicha resignation over doctors' strike

Azimio la Umoja had called on the resignation of the CS over her inability to resolve the doctors' strike.

National Assembly Minority Leader Opiyo Wandayi accused Nakhumicha of incompetency and treating the doctors' strike casually.
