Outfits With Boots: 6 Looks for Any Occasion

April 15, 2024
blog

When it’s warm outside, we always slip into a pair of sandals or flip-flops to run out of the house. And once it gets a tad chillier, we opt for closed-toe shoes like comfortable flats. But now that we’re bracing for cooler temperatures, the go-to shoe year after year during the colder months is a pair of boots. From ankle booties to mid-calf and knee-high boots, the shoe style is a staple item in every woman’s wardrobe. And it’s possible that you already have a few pairs but aren’t sure how to style them or you’re looking for new ways to wear them. Maybe you’re in the market for a new pair of boots — no matter the case, we turned to celebrity stylist Eric Himel for the best outfits with boots that will have you feeling great and turning heads.

What to look for in a pair of boots

When it comes to selecting a pair of boots, there are a few key things you should look for:

1. Keep comfort in mind

Long gone are the days of slipping your feet into uncomfortable, painful heels or boots just for the sake of fashion. There are many options on the market today that are both comfortable and stylish, ensuring your feet are pain-free all day. For added comfort, look for soles that are cushioned or padded for additional support.

2. Make sure the heel is sturdy

The height of the heel on a boot is completely up to personal preference. But no matter how high or short, you want something that is sturdy and stable. This doesn’t mean you have to choose chunky, block-heel boots — it just means that if going for a thinner heel, make sure it feels secure enough to the sole and isn’t so skinny you’re going twist your ankle. That’s why stylists like Himel advise doing a test walk around the house before venturing outside in pair of boots.

However, you don’t want to completely ditch the heel when it comes to picking out a boot. “When buying a flat boot, avoid totally flat ones to not appear elfin, and opt for one with a ½” heel instead,” adds Himel.

3. Choose a pair that’s versatile

While a bold, brightly hued pair of boots is always fun once in a while (see below for a vivid red pair), overall, it’s best to choose a pair that you can wear with almost any outfit. If you tend to wear more blacks and darker colors, opt for a pair of black boots. And if you wear more browns and tans, consider brown boots. Himel says it’s great to have one pair of each (black and brown), but boots that work well with the color scheme you wear most is a good place to start.
