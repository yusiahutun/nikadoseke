French-girl favourite Szane reflects on a decade in fashion

March 18, 2024
blog

When it comes to understanding the world of sustainable fashion, it can be tough to know where to start, but there are plenty of labels doing amazing things in the space. In our monthly series Unstitched, we focus on a brand that knows exactly what it means to be a sustainable force for good in fashion today.

This time, we’re getting to know Sézane, a Parisian brand that is leading by example in the responsible fashion world with over a decade of experience in the industry.

In today's climate, prioritising sustainable and responsible materials and methods of production is a must for any fashion brand, but this is something that Parisian label Sézane has been thinking about since it first launched 10 years ago. Over the past decade, the ready-to-wear brand – famed for its French cool-girl feel and vintage-inspired aesthetic – has set a great example of how to adapt to the changing industry.

The label first came to be when Morgane Sézalory discovered a suitcase of abandoned vintage clothing. She had some of these pieces altered and adjusted, and started selling them online through her first business venture, which was called Les Composantes.

Once she did this, there was no going back, Sézalory had found her passion, and by 2013, she had launched her own clothing label, Sézane, which was inspired by vintage fashion.

Sézane

Now, a decade into the business, Sézalory's intentions remain the same as they were that first day: there is a focus on quality over quantity. "The pieces we shop today should be the vintage of tomorrow," she believes. In order to do this, the label produces timeless high-quality pieces that truly stand the test of time, expertly crafted in its Parisian ateliers from eco-friendly and innovative materials.

The label's years of hard work haven't gone unnoticed – Sézane is officially B-corp certified, meaning it's a label you can trust have prioritised the right working conditions for its creators, minimised its environmental footprint and has focused on producing its collections in the most eco-friendly way possible.

We sat down with Sézalory to discuss what she thinks makes a sustainable brand, how she thinks the industry should change and why she's hopeful for the future.

What makes a truly sustainable brand?

"Instead of simply creating environmentally friendly pieces, sustainable brands should be changing the way we perceive and treat our clothes. At Sézane, we believe that the pieces we buy today should become the vintage of tomorrow. Therefore, we design each Sézane piece like a unique and rare work of art, crafting them in the finest materials, with the expertise of our partner ateliers. We sincerely hope that our clients will wear our pieces season after season and can eventually pass them down to the next generation.

Sézane

"Sézane is based on responsible stock volumes to avoid overproduction. Instead of regular flash sales and discounts, we release our archives twice a year with pieces from our previous collections at slightly reduced prices. The idea is to find a home for all our creations, without overwhelming our clients. After all, the most sustainable pieces are the ones that we already own and can continue to wear for years to come, thanks to their durability and timeless design."

What do you think needs to change in the fashion industry?

"There are many things that need to change, but I'm hopeful that we’re heading in the right direction. Since the beginning of Sézane, we have sought to be transparent with our clients and communicate our sustainability efforts. More and more brands are becoming transparent about their sustainability efforts with their clients."

What do you want to achieve personally with your brand within the next 10 years, in terms of sustainability?

"To keep increasing our quality to ensure the durability of our products. At the moment, we’re also sourcing materials like linen and silk; there are so many more things to imagine, but we are keeping our goals in mind and we’ll try to work with the same enthusiasm and passion for making it happen."

How have our attitudes changed toward fashion, since you started Sézane 10 years ago?

"I think customers are more aware of their own responsibility in the change they can bring to fashion. They are now thinking more before buying something, they’re more aware of fabrics and production than they were before. Customers are looking more towards vintage, when I first started out as Les Composantes, thrifted pieces weren't that common."

What advice would you give to those wanting to make their business sustainable?

"It’s easier to start a sustainable brand than transform an existing brand. I started Les Composantes and then Sézane with only the production of small drops and then we decided to make some changes to our fabrics, for example, all our denim is now 100 per cent sustainable, made from GOTs-certified cotton. Don’t try to do it for communication purposes or status but actually try to make a difference."

Sézane

What’s the smallest change a consumer could make to become more eco-conscious?

"Take care of the pieces that are already in your wardrobe. Our clothes are often extensions of ourselves; they express a part of our personality and encapsulate moments of our lives. The pieces I love the most are the ones my mother has handed down to me. I’ve worn them a lot over the years, and have always taken the time to repair or alter them so I can one day hand them down to my daughters. The pieces I care the most about are the ones my mother gave me. I cherish them and I wear them all the time – it’s the most sustainable way to approach clothes."

What are the most common inaccurate “facts” about sustainability you see pushed out?

"The fact that something made in organic cotton is necessarily sustainable. It actually depends on so many things: where it is made, how, and with which fabric. We only work with the most concrete certifications like GOTs, Oeko-Tex, RWS."

Where do you turn to when you feel confused about sustainability and need more detail?

"I have a team of experts dedicated to sustainability, and they are the best ones to answer my questions."
