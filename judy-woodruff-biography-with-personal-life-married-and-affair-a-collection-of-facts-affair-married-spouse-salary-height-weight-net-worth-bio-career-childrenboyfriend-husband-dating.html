Short Description of Judy Woodruff

Judith Woodruff, aka Judy Woodruff, is a native of America. She serves as an anchor for PBS NewsHour. Besides anchoring, Judy is famous as a journalist and writer. In her career, Judy has worked for several television organizations, such as CNN, NBC News, and PBS.

Besides that, Woodruff is also a member of the Council on Foreign Relations and a board member of the International Women’s Media Foundation. Judy Woodruff is a reputed television personality with a huge amount of income.

Early Life and Education Of Judy Woodruff (Age, Ethnicity, Nationality)

Recalling her early life, Judy was born Judith Woodruff on November 20, 1946, in Tulsa, Oklahoma, the U.S.A. She is a daughter of Anna Lee (Payne) Woodruff and William H. Woodruff. She belongs to American nationality and concerning her ethnicity she belongs to a white ethnic group. As of 2018, her age is 72 years old.

Regarding her education, she graduated from the Academy of Richmond County and has earned a degree in Political Science from Duke University.

Judy Woodruff’s Career

Since the year 1970, Judy started her professional career of Journalist. She started working as a news anchor at WAGA-TV. Later, she served the news cover regarding the 1976 U.S. presidential campaign for NBC. Later, she was promoted to the chief White House correspondent for NBC News. In the year 1993, she joined CNN and hosted Inside Politics for 12 long years.

Then, she shifted toward PBS, to work on Generation Next (documentary about American young people and their characteristics, values, and thoughts on family, faith, and politics). Besides working as a Journalist, Judi is also an author. Woodruff has published a book titled, This is Judy Woodruff at the White House.
