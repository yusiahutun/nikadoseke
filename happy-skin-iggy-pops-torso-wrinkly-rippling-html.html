His torso may now be more wrinkly than rippling – but Iggy Pop clearly has no intention of adding a shirt to his stage wardrobe.

The 64-year-old, who has been performing bare-chested since finding fame with The Stooges in the 1960s, showed he has no plans to either calm down or cover up when he appeared at the Isle of Wight festival on Sunday.

And with the line-up including 71-year-old Tom Jones, he wasn’t the only veteran act entertaining the 70,000 crowd.

Iggy Pop, who has been performing bare-chested since finding fame with The Stooges in the 1960s, appeared at the Isle of Wight festival on Sunday

This year's headliners were Kings of Leon, Foo Fighters and Kasabian.

Day one saw Kings Of Leon and Kaiser Chiefs, whereas Saturday saw Seasick Steve, Stornoway, Lissie, Mike and the Mechanics, Hurts, before Foo Fighters and Pulp topped the bill.

Kasabian rounded off the bash on Sunday after the likes of Beady Eye, The Script, Plan B and special guest Jeff Beck had strutted their stuff on stage.

The site was plagued by heavy rain on the final day, but many revellers decided to make the most of the conditions and play around in the mud

Liam Gallagher of Beady Eye struts his stuff on stage during the final day of the Isle of Wight Festival on Sunday

Gavin & Stacey actor Mathew Horne also made an appearance, showing off his DJ skills at the Carling Local on Saturday night

Although the weather had been good for the first two days of the festival, the site was plagued by heavy rain on the final day.

Many revellers decided to make the most of the conditions however and play around in the mud.

Meanwhile, Gavin & Stacey actor Mathew Horne also made an appearance, showing off his DJ skills at the Carling Local.

He played a selection of tunes to campers in the know who headed down to the after-party on Saturday night at the venue, which was handily located right near the campsite.
