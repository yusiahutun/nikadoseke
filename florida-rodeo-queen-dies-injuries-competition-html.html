Florida rodeo queen Lara Dewees dies after accident during barrel racing competition

September 25, 2024
blog

A rodeo queen from Florida has died days after an incident sent her flying over her horse's head during a competition.

Lara Dewees, 27, of Pasco Country, was fatally injured at an event in Hattiesburg, Mississippi, last month, the Tampa Tribune reported.

Her horse, Genny, fell, causing Dewees to drop the reins. Genny then headed towards the arena's exit gate after circling the barrels.

The horse was wearing special shoes made to walk on dust specifically. The gate should have been closed to keep Genny away from the asphalt, Dewees's granddmother Jeanette Sasser told the Tampa Tribune.

Lara Dewees (pictured with her horse Genny), 27, of Pasco County, Florida, died from her injuries after an accident during a competition in Hattiesburg, Mississippi. Her horse fell, causing Dewees to drop the reins. Genny headed towards the exit and slipped on the asphalt, sending Dewees flying and landing on her face

But Genny exited the area and slipped on the ground. Dewees, who couldn't grab the reins again, flew over Genny's head and landed on her face.

She had surgery to remove a blood clot from her brain and her facial bones were broken, including her eye socket, Sasser told the Tampa Tribune.

Dewees, who had an 18-month-old son, Laytin, was planning to marry the boy's father, rodeo rider Cory Moesching.

Dewees (pictured) got her first horse when she was three and started riding before she could walk

He visited her in the hospital and, as her health seemed to improve, even talked of tying the knot there.

But days after her fall, Dewees's condition declined. Doctors believe she died due to another blood clot.

'She rode into glory,' Sasser told the Tampa Tribune. 'Lara was our life. I don’t know how we’re going to go on without her.'

Dewees, a national barrel racing champion, was part of the third generation of rodeo competitors in her family.

At the time of her accident, she was trying to retain her 2015 title as the Women's Professional Rodeo Association Southeastern Circuit Divisional Tour Finals Reserve Champion.

Dewees, a celebrity in her hometown of fewer than 2,000 people, had been crowned Miss Teen Rodeo Florida 2007 and has survived cancer.

She got her first horse when she was three and began riding before she could even walk. 

Her uncle, Spunk Sasser, 50, is the second most decorated cowboy in the Southeastern Rodeo Circuit.

Her father, Steve Dewees, did calf roping, a type of rodeo where the rider loops a rope around a calf while riding a horse, then ties the calf's legs together.

Dewees's mother, Lisa Belcher, was a barrel racer like her, meaning they took their horses through a cloverleaf-shaped circuit made out of barrels in a race against time.

Her barrel-racing friends will follow behind her casket on their horses during Dewees's funeral on Sunday.
