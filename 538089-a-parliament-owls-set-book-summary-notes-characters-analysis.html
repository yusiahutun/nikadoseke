A Parliament of Owls setbook is set in the bird kingdom ruled by owls, especially through the powerful Parliament of Owls and the Royal Trees. The king of this kingdom is known as King Tula Nyongoro, the Royal Owl, who rules with an iron arm.

A Parliament of Owls characters

A character is a person in a book or story. Writers use characters to create dialogue and perform actions which move the story along a plotline. Several characters in the Parliament of Owl novel represent various facets of the power struggle and societal dynamics within the bird kingdom.

They directly contribute to the narrative's overarching themes of governance, corruption, and rebellion. Some of the notable characters include:

  • King Tula Nyongoro: The Royal Owl who rules over the bird kingdom from the Royal Trees. He represents authority and power in the Parliament of Owls. He portrays a dictatorial type of leadership in the kingdom, with his oppressive rule leading to rebellion.
  • Money Bags Owl: This owl represents corruption in the setting. He works closely with his ally, King Tula Nyongoro, who is known for his greed and exploiting resources for personal gain.
  • Socialite Owl: Sponsored by Money Bags Owl, she uses her position in the Parliament of Owls to advance frivolous bills and maintain the status quo.
  • Iron Lady Owl: Leads the rebellion against the corrupt governance of the Parliament of Owls, advocating for justice and equality.
  • Oyundi the Fire-finch: A cunning and strategic bird who becomes a central figure in the rebellion against the Royal Owl dictatorship. He is well trained by his mentor, the cunning Ogila Nyarondo.
  • Red String Puppet Owl: A sycophantic character who colludes with Socialite Owl and others to maintain the oppressive regime of the Parliament of Owls. He is depicted as selfish and self-centred and has no stand.
  • Veteran P: He is the one who leads the parrots in reporting on the events unfolding in the bird kingdom. This character serves as a voice for truth and transparency in the kingdom, helping them make better plans.
  • Arum Tidie: Arum acts as the chief propagandist for the Parliament of Owls. Upon his death, the birds of this kingdom become uneasy, and momentum for change within the kingdom begins.

A Parliament of Owls analysis

What is the novel a Parliament of Owls about? The popular set book satirises poor leadership, tribalism, and corruption, showing how they hinder societal progress. It also demonstrates that marginalised individuals, like Oyundi the Fire-finch, can spark change peacefully.

Importantly, it encourages youths and Kenyans to engage in civic awareness and drive positive change. There are several themes arising from the play, such as:

1. Corruption and abuse of power (dictatorship)

The play depicts a society where corruption runs rampant, particularly within the Parliament of Owls. Characters like Money Bags Owl exploit their positions for personal gain, grabbing territory and sponsoring frivolous bills. This theme highlights the negative consequences of unchecked power and greed.

Power is also abused when the leaders in the book use dictatorship to get what they need from the commoners. Commoners feel insecure about their future and safety, knowing they are at the mercy of the owl-dominated Parliament of Owls.

2. Love

The author of this book tackles the theme of love from all angles, whereby there is a twist of laughter, lies and illusion. Throughout the story, there is a sense of companionship and solidarity among the rebel birds and their supporters. Some characters, like Iron Lady Owl and Oyundi the Fire-finch, demonstrate a deep love for justice and fairness.

Love is also explored through themes of conflict and betrayal. The corrupt actions of characters like Money Bags Owl and Socialite Owl reveal a betrayal of trust and a lack of love for their fellow birds.

3. Favoritism

This theme is evident in the kingdom's unequal treatment of different bird species. Those in power like Money Bags Owl and Socialite Owl, show favouritism towards their kind, passing laws and making decisions that benefit owls at the expense of other birds.

4. Oppression and rebellion

Oppression is a central theme, as the corrupt governance of the owls suppresses dissenting voices and exploits other bird species. However, this oppression sparks rebellion among the oppressed, led by characters like Iron Lady Owl and supported by a coalition of birds seeking change. The narrative explores the dynamics of resistance against unjust authority.

5. Political satire and critique

The play critiques real-world political systems through its satirical lens, highlighting issues such as bad governance, tribalism, and corruption. By exaggerating and parodying these issues, the narrative encourages reflection and discussion on governance and leadership.

6. Equality

A contrasting theme emerges through the rebellion led by characters like Oyundi the Fire-finch, who advocates for a more inclusive government. The narrative champions equality and inclusivity, suggesting that all birds should have a voice in governance, regardless of species or status. This theme stirs hope in a situation that seems difficult to many.
