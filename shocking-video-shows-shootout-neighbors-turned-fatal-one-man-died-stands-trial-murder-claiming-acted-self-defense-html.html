Video shows Las Vegas shootout between Giancarlo John Banova and his neighbour

March 23, 2024
blog

A shocking shootout between two neighbors that left one dead and the other on trial for his murder was all captured on video.

James Stapleton of Las Vegas, Nevada can be seen pointing and shooting a gun at Giancarlo Banova as he backs out of his driveway on May 7 while the two men argue.

Banova, who also fired his gun, was killed, and Stapleton is now being tried for his murder while claiming he acted in self defense. 

James Stapleton and Giancarlo John Banova engaged in a shootout outside their homes on May 7 (Stapleton in red holding  a rifle)

Banova (left) was killed and Stapleton (right) is now on trial for murder

Banova's surveillance camera captured the incident, but it is unclear who fired first (Banova holding a handgun in his truck above)

The Las Vegas Review-Journal reports that an ex-girlfriend of Stapleton testified in court on Monday that he and Banova had an 'ongoing feud,' and that he even texted her right before the shooting.

'I think I’m going to put an end to my problem with my neighbor,' Stapleton texted Ticia Lewdon that morning, according to her testimony.

'He's done.'

Stapleton then sent another text that read; 'I have the bullets, there won’t be any mistakes. I’m waiting for him and not hiding it, so when the cops ask, don’t lie.'

Video shows Banova backing out of his driveway on the day of the shooting while arguing with Stapleton, who is standing outside with his parents.

As Banova moves from his driveway into the street, Stapleton suddenly reaches into the passenger side of his car and pulls out a 'long rifle' while walking towards the Banova's car.

Banova then drives towards Stapleton, at which point he begins firing his rifle.

Banova begins firing back with a handgun, though it is not clear if that happens before or after Stapleton shoots at him. 

Nancy Lemcke, Stapleton's attorney, claims Banova fired first.

There is no sound on the video to determine exactly who fired the first shot.

Banova died a week later on May 14, while Stapleton was treated for minor wounds and released from the hospital.
