Meaning of Pulk/pull (true Love Waits Version) by Radiohead

July 26, 2024
blog

In Radiohead's enigmatic and thought-provoking song "Pulk/pull (true Love Waits Version)", the band delves deep into the complexities of relationships and the lengths one might go to please their significant other. The overarching theme of the song seems to revolve around the sacrifices we are willing to make in the name of love, even if it means compromising our own beliefs and identities.One of the standout lyrics in the song is "I'll drown my beliefs to have your babies." This line speaks to the idea of surrendering one's own principles and personal convictions for the sake of a relationship. It suggests a willingness to let go of one's own identity in order to conform and please the other person. This theme explores the notion that love can sometimes lead us to make sacrifices that may not align with our true selves.Another striking lyric is "I'll dress like your niece and wash your swollen feet." This line conjures up images of extreme devotion and caretaking. It delves into the idea that love can compel us to take on various roles and responsibilities to make our partner happy, even if it means stepping into unconventional roles or doing tasks that may seem peculiar. It explores the theme of selflessness and the lengths one might go to demonstrate their affection and commitment.The repetition of the plea "Don't leave, don't leave" in the chorus further emphasizes the theme of desperation and fear of abandonment. It delves into the fragility of relationships and the insecurities that can arise when one feels their love is being threatened. This theme speaks to the deeply human need for connection and the fear of losing that connection."I'm not living, I'm just killing time" is another powerful lyric that explores the theme of longing and emptiness. It suggests a sense of dissatisfaction and the idea that love, or the absence of it, can impact our overall sense of purpose and fulfillment. This line evokes a sense of yearning for something more meaningful and highlights the inherent vulnerability and longing that can come with being in love.Throughout the song, Radiohead masterfully weaves together these intricate and unexpected themes to create a haunting narrative on the complexities of love and the sacrifices we are willing to make for it. "Pulk/pull (true Love Waits Version)" ultimately asks listeners to reflect on the depths of their own emotions and the ways in which love can both elevate and challenge our sense of self. It is a song that touches on the universal struggles and paradoxes of love, reminding us that it is not always simple or straightforward, but always worth exploring and understanding.
