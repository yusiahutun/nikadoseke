Student who developed cystic acne after laser hair removal reveals how she healed her scars

Patrice Conditsis first noticed tiny lumps forming on her face two years ago, after having a few laser hair removal treatments.

The 18-year-old from Sydney, Australia, had only wanted to get rid of the black hairs around her side burns, cheeks and upper lip when acne began to form.

'After the third session I started noticing that I would breakout every time I would get laser done,' she said in a YouTube video. 'I ignored it and kept doing laser until it was the sixth or seventh session.'

Can laser hair removal trigger acne?

'Breakouts often arise from laser hair removal because the hair is dense and coarse. Afterwards, a portion of the hair follicles contain dead hair fragments, which the body tries to expel,' facial plastic surgeon Kelly Bomer said.

'When this happens, there is an inflammatory response, which can cause red bumps with pus, resembling a red pimple with a whitehead.'

To stop it from happening, it's recommended you use an antibacterial ointment or gentle skin-repairing post procedure.

She also suggests using a light moisturiser instead of a heavy one or using an antibiotic prescribed by a doctor to clear up any existing inflammation prior to the procedure.

What are Patrice's top tips for curing acne?

1. Change your pillowcase regularly. For three days Patrice lays on one side of the pillow, before switching to sleep three days on the other and then washing it.

2. Don't pick at your face. It's spreading bacteria and you're just creating scars.

3. Clean your phone. There is so much bacteria on your phone so make sure to wipe it down daily. 

4. Drink celery juice and lemon juice once a day.

5. Wear aloe vera facial masks once a day and clay masks once per week.

She was offered a number of prescription medications to try and heal her skin but decided she didn't want to 'mask' the problem, but treat it at the core.

'The first thing I started doing six months ago was drinking lemon water as soon as I wake up because it's packed with vitamin C,' she said.

Fifteen minutes later she will make herself a fresh celery juice from 7-8 stems blended.

'I take off the leaves before I juice it because I find it's less bitter. But I still battle with the taste every morning,' she said.

She then waits another 15 minutes before having breakfast and ensures she's drinking two litres of water a day to continue the detox process.

Her diet doesn't include soy, sugar, sesame or dairy products, which she found to inflame her skin.

In terms of products she picks pure aloe vera from her garden, cuts it in half and slathers the antibacterial liquid onto her face as a mask each night.

Patrice sleeps in the mask, which dries clear, and washes it off in the morning to further reduce inflammation.

Once a week she applies the Aztec Secret Indian Healing Clay ($20), which is known as the 'world's most powerful facial', to draw out impurities and skin debris.  

While she doesn't believe her skin is 'perfect' as yet, the incredible difference she has noticed in the last six months has convinced her this is the best routine going forward.
