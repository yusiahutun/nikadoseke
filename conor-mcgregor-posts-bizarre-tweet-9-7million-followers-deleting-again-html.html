While the post was quickly deleted, there are still dozens of replies on Twitter. One user said: 'Nobody understands this tweet'.


And another joked: 'Where'd your tweet go? It was deleted before I could like it'. 

Earlier this week, McGregor posted photos of himself showing off his watches and 'choking' himself by gripping his own neck.

The posts were deleted but have resurfaced in what is becoming a common theme with McGregor.

McGregor posted another strange Tweet on Thursday morning with the caption: 'Always wear your seatbelt.'

And on Thursday morning he posted a picture of himself driving with the caption: 'Always wear your seatbelt.' 

He also recently posted a photo on Instagram of him smoking in the back seat of a car with his fiancee along with the caption: 'Who's drivin, Not us'.

The UFC star has not fought in over 17 months since he suffered a first round defeat to Dustin Poirier after breaking his tibia.

Last month, he compared himself to Arnold Schwarzenegger and Bruce Lee after showing off his latest body transformation on social media.

The 34-year-old, shared the image on his social media accounts, with the post on Facebook captioned: 'If @schwarzenegger and @brucelee had a mad Irish baby'.

McGregor (pictured) shared a clip of himself looking like an ape and saying, 'I told you I'd be back' last month

The image included the former world champion sat on a bike tensing as he continues to tease a return to the octagon. It was also shared on Twitter and Instagram before being deleted from the latter.

And just weeks before that, McGregor shared a clip of himself looking like an ape - before later claiming he will become Ireland's highest-grossing movie star.

McGregor is currently filming the reboot of 1980s classic Road House with Hollywood star Jake Gyllenhaal.

Shooting commenced in August and the Amazon Prime-produced movie is set to be released in early 2023.
