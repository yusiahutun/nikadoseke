Rodney Atkins hits the road on “These Are My People Tour,” celebrating his first new music in five years. The tour kicks off April 18 in Patchogue, N.Y., and contunes through June 29 in Rosemont, Ill. Fans can find more information and purchase tickets at www.rodneyatkins.com.

“Even during my time away from the studio, we were always out on the road with our fans,” says Atkins. “They still sing along to every word of ‘Take A Back Road’ or ‘Watching You’ as if those songs are brand new, so I’m excited to get back out there and add some fresh music to the mix.”

The Magpie Salute, which brings together Black Crowes duo Rich Robinson and Marc Ford, bassist Sven Pipien (also from the Crowes) along with lead singer John Hogg (Hookah Brown, Moke) and drummer Joe Magistro have announced upcoming dates in support of their yet-to-be-released debut studio album. Six of the shows are with the Gov’t Mule / Avett Brothers co-headlining tour.

Tickets are available at www.themagpiesalute.com. The band has already toured 75 gigs in 2017.

More dates are expected to be announced.

Neko Case, with 40 international dates already on the books, has announced a series of headlining gigs in North America for September, including New York’s Beacon Theatre and a dozen more stops to support her forthcoming album Hell-On, out June 1.

The album’s second single, “Bad Luck,” came out last week and was dubbed by Rolling Sone as “an indie rock gem.”
