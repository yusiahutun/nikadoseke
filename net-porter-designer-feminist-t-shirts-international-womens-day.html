Net-a-Porter launches feminist T-shirts to mark International Women's Day

July 13, 2024
blog

To mark International Women's Day on Friday March 8, Net-a-Porter has launched six exclusive T-shirts with all proceeds benefitting non-profit charity, Women for Women International.

The online retailer has teamed up with designers Victoria Beckham, Isabel Marant, Rosie Assoulin and Alexa Chung for the project with female-led brands, Ellery and Perfect Moment, also making the line-up.

Related Story

Each T-shirt represents what female empowerment means to each brand, with prices ranging from £85 to £200.

For instance, Victoria Beckham's £175 white tee reads, "I am her" in the designer's own handwriting.

On the project, the mother-of-four said: "It’s more important than ever that women support each other and hopefully this aids in raising awareness for a subject close to my heart."

Assoulin's T-shirt depicts a red heart crafted from binary code.

Together, the numbers translate to: "Happy International Women's Day". On Instagram, the designer explained that she hopes to promote "coding and tech literacy and education for girls and women".

All profits from the collection will go towards non-profit organisation, Women for Women International.

The charity helps female survivors of war rebuild their lives by enrolling them on a year-long training course where they will learn about their rights and health whilst also being given the opportunity to master a vocational skill.

Related Story

The aim of the project is also to unite a sisterhood of women with similar experiences in some of the world's most dangerous places.

Since its launch in 1993, the organisation has helped nearly half a million women by providing them with the tools and resources to combat poverty.
