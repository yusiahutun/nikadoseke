20 most beautiful cities in Africa to visit (with pictures)

April 04, 2024
blog

Africa is steeped in rich history, unexplored lands, and amazing cultures. Combining these factors makes the continent one of the most interesting places one can visit today. From the surrounding idyllic beaches to sprawling mountainsides and beautiful architecture, the most beautiful cities in Africa have an amazing appeal to the modern-day traveller.

The most beautiful cities in Africa include old towns that have retained their originality since the colonial era to ones that have undergone massive modernization.

Which are the most beautiful cities in Africa?

Here is a look at the top 20 beautiful cities in Africa.

20. Chefchaouen

  • Country: Morocco
  • Population: 42,786
  • Famous for: Unique, blue-rinsed houses and amazing landscapes

Chefchaouen is widely known as Morocco's Blue City. The city is located up the Moroccan Rif Mountains, providing visitors with amazing views of the surrounding landscape. The city's houses have a predominantly blue colour, resulting in a picturesque view, especially during sunsets.

Besides the unforgettable views and numerous attractions, Chefchaouen also has a rich heritage.

19. Johannesburg

  • Country: South Africa
  • Population: 5.635 million
  • Famous for: The picturesque mountainsides and nearby gold mines

Johannesburg was established in 1886 after the discovery of gold in the surrounding areas. The city is situated on the Highveld, the world-famous grassy plateau that sweeps across the country's interior. On the other side, Johannesburg faces the Witwatersrand, a block of low-lying rocky ridges.

Johannesburg has a vibrant entertainment scene and excellent African cuisine, making it one of the best cities in Africa.

18. Algiers

  • Country: Algeria
  • Population: 3.7 million
  • Famous for: French-inspired architecture

Algiers is the capital city of Algeria in North Africa. The city is built on the slopes of the Sahel Hills, which lie parallel to the Mediterranean Sea coast and extend more than 15 kilometres along the Bay of Algiers. The modern architecture in Algiers glows in dazzling white during sunrise and sunset, dominating the harbour and the bay.

17. Windhoek

  • Country: Namibia
  • Population: 431,000
  • Famous for: Remarkable German architecture

Windhoek is Namibia’s capital. It is a lively metropolis that is remarkably neat and orderly. This trait has always been attributed to the country's association with its colonial master, Germany.

Windhoek is situated in a beautiful valley and features buildings reminiscent of the German architectural style. The German presence is also felt strongly in the food, restaurants, and, unsurprisingly, beer. The Namibian beer brewed in Windhoek is often said to be the best in Africa.

16. Lamu

  • Country: Kenya
  • Population: 25,385
  • Famous for: Pristine beaches

Lamu is an island town in the Indian Ocean off the East African coast. It is situated approximately 241 kilometres northeast of Mombasa. This city's architecture is an intricate blend of Swahili, Persian, Indian, European, and Arabic cultures.

Lamu is renowned for its narrow winding streets, old stone houses, beautiful doorways, and marvellous beaches.

15. Bahir Dar

  • Country: Ethiopia
  • Population: 768,899
  • Famous for: Wide streets lined with palm trees and colourful flowers

Bahir Dar is the capital of the Amhara region, located in northern Ethiopia. The city is situated on the outskirts of Lake Tana, 600 kilometres from Addis Ababa. Bahir Dar is the third-largest city in Ethiopia and, inarguably, the most beautiful.

This marvellous city is home to historic monasteries, charming lodges, exotic fish markets, beautiful lakes and the world-famous Blue Nile Falls.

14. Gaborone

  • Country: Botswana
  • Population: 232,000
  • Famous for: The Gaborone Game Reserve

Gaborone is a buzzing and fast-growing city named after Kgosi Gaborone, leader of the Batlokwa people. The name Gaborone means 'it does not fit badly' in reference to the location where the Batlokwa people chose to build the city.

13. Luxor

  • Country: Egypt
  • Population: 1.328 million
  • Famous for: Many ancient Egyptian monuments

Over the years, Luxor has been known by many names, including Waset, Ta Ipet, and Thebes. When it was known as Thebes, it served as the capital of the ancient Egyptian kingdom. Today the city is widely known as the world's greatest open-air museum and is home to some of Egypt's most famous monuments, tombs, and temples.

12. Nairobi

  • Country: Kenya
  • Population: 4.397 million
  • Famous for: The Nairobi National Park

Kenya's capital, Nairobi, is often termed The City in the Sun, and a visit can be as pleasant as that sounds. Visitors come to Nairobi for various reasons, some of the most common being business and animal viewing and culture trips.

The city is only a short flight from spectacular wildlife parks such as the Maasai Mara, Amboseli, and Ol Pejeta. The city has also become a magnet for some of the world's biggest corporations trying to gain a foothold in Africa.

11. Dar es Salaam

  • Country: Tanzania
  • Population: 4.365 million
  • Famous for: The laid-back seaside setting

Dar es Salaam is the largest city, busiest industrial centre, and the largest port in Tanzania. The city has numerous attractions, including pristine beaches, monuments, artisan centres, shopping centres, restaurants, nightclubs, museums, and botanical gardens.

If you are looking for a nice, somewhat laid-back coastal city in Africa, Dar es Salaam should definitely be on your shortlist.

10. Agadir

  • Country: Morocco
  • Population: 421,844
  • Famous for: A pristine beach with 340 days of sunshine per year

Agadir is a beautiful city along Morocco's Atlantic coast. Nestled in the foothills of the Anti-Atlas Mountains, Agadir is one of the most famous tourist destinations in Morocco. The city is renowned for its golf courses, wide crescent beach and busy seaside lined with cafes, restaurants, and bars.

9. Franschhoek

  • Country: South Africa
  • Population: 17,000
  • Famous for: superb local cuisine and exceptional wine

Franschhoek is a small city in South Africa's Western Cape with hundreds of years old vineyards. The town also features beautiful Dutch architecture. Numerous trails wind through flowers and wildlife at the nearby Mont Rochelle Nature Reserve, opening to views of the legendary Franschhoek Valley.

Franschhoek is renowned for its fine wine and superb local cuisine, making it the finest city in Africa for high-class wining and dining.

8. Aswan

  • Country: Egypt
  • Population: 1.568 million
  • Famous for: Warm climate and Egyptian archaeological sites

Aswan is an Egyptian city located on the banks of the Nile River, and one of the top 10 beautiful cities in Africa. For centuries, it was southern Egypt's strategic and commercial gateway. Like numerous other Egyptian cities, Aswan contains significant archaeological sites like the Philae temple complex.

Aswan's environment is characterized by a warm climate, making it one of the best African cities to visit when escaping winter.

7. Djenne

  • Country: Mali
  • Population: 32,944
  • Famous for: The Great Mosque of Djenne: The world's largest mud-brick structure

Djenné is a town and an urban commune in the Niger Delta region of central Mali. The town is among the oldest trading cities and centres of Muslim scholarship in Mali. It is situated on the fertile flood lands between the Bani and Niger rivers.

This ancient city and its environs are home to numerous ruins, remains, and buildings that are culturally and historically significant, the most notable being the Great Mosque of Djenne, the world's largest mud-brick structure.

6. Cape Town

  • Country: South Africa
  • Population: 4.618 million
  • Famous for: The Robben Island Museum and the surrounding Table Mountains

Cape Town is part of the world-famous Garden Route, the 200-kilometre stretch of coast connecting Mossel Bay to the Storms River Mouth. The Garden Route is arguably one of the most iconic and charming places in the world to sample outstanding wines and eat excellent food.

The stretch sports beautiful coastal views, picturesque lakes and gorgeous farmlands, ingredients for the perfect, unforgettable road trip. Cape Town is widely regarded as the best city in Africa due to its unrivalled combination of tourist attraction features and the great quality of life.

5. Essaouira

  • Country: Morocco
  • Population: 77,966
  • Famous for: Numerous riads (a type of bed-and-breakfast)

Essaouira is a beautiful port city and resort on Morocco's Atlantic Ocean coast. Its medina (old town) is protected by 18th-century seafront ramparts called the Skala de la Kasbah, designed by European engineers decades ago.

Old brass cannons line the city's walls, guarding against the strong trade winds. These winds make Essaouira's beaches some of the best for kitesurfing.

4. Durban

  • Country: South Africa
  • Population: 3.721 million
  • Famous for: World-class surf spots and beaches

Durban was formerly known as Port Natal and is the largest city in KwaZulu-Natal province. The city's location on Natal Bay in the Indian Ocean makes it one of the most iconic cities in South Africa. It is overlooked to the south by the Bluff (the majestic hills separating the bay from the sea) and stretches across the Umgeni River.

Durban is considered one of the best kitesurfing locations in Africa.

3. Marrakech

  • Country: Morocco
  • Population: 928,850
  • Famous for: Excellent cuisine

Marrakech is the perfect blend of modernity and tradition. Commonly known as the red city, Marrakech offers visitors an unrivalled change of scenery. Horse-drawn carriage rides, sunny terraces, travelling artists and other day and night activities give visitors unforgettable experiences.

Marrakech is also widely known as the city of luxury, thanks to its famous palaces, charming riads (traditional houses), 5-star hotels, and luxury spas.

2. Cairo

  • Country: Egypt
  • Population: 9.54 million
  • Famous for: The nearby pyramids of Giza

Cairo is the capital city of Egypt and one of the most magnificent African cities today. The city is set on the banks of the Nile River and is home to Tahrir Square and the vast Egyptian Museum. There are also numerous Egyptian artefacts, including royal mummies and gilded King Tutankhamun remains. Cairo is also close to Giza, the site of the iconic pyramids and Great Sphinx, dating to the 26th century BC.

Besides being remarkably beautiful, Cairo is also among the biggest cities in Africa.

1. Stone Town

  • Country: Zanzibar
  • Population: 16,000
  • Famous for: Blend of cultures including Swahili, Arab, Persian, Indian and European elements.

The Stone Town in Zanzibar is also known as Mji Mkongwe and makes up the larger part of the old Zanzibar City. The newer portion of the city is now known as Ng'ambo, which loosely translates to 'the other side.

Stone Town is located on the western coast of Zanzibar and features an amazing, rich history, a beautiful maze of ancient streets and alleyways that form the westernmost tip of Zanzibar. The combination of rich culture, beautiful beaches, and friendly natives make Stone Town one of the most amazing African cities today.

The most beautiful cities in Africa have numerous factors that contribute to their appeal. These include picturesque surroundings, beautiful infrastructure, and majestic physical features. These cities continue to increase the continent's appeal as a prime tourist destination.
