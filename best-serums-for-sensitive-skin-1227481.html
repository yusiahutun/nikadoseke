We know how frustrating it can be when your skin reacts badly to a product that works wonders for others but, finding the best serum for sensitive skin should never be that hard. Before you try any random product, understanding the type of skin you have and the product that best suits it is an extremely important education you need to have. Any use of the wrong product could cause a bad chemical reaction, thereby damaging your skin. You might not notice it right away, but in the long run your skin barriers will weaken and cause a lot of problems. Serums with ingredients like vitamin C and hyaluronic acid can protect your skin from dullness, dark spots, or even aging signs that can be caused by harsh environments. Since everyone’s skin reacts differently to different products, you need to pick the one that works the best for your skin.

“When it comes to sensitive skin, it's essential to choose skincare ingredients that are gentle, soothing, and unlikely to cause irritation. While individual sensitivities can vary, some ingredients are generally considered safe and well-tolerated by sensitive skin types”, says Dr. B.R.N. Padmini, a serving consultant in the plastic surgery unit of CARE Hospitals, Hyderabad.

Laying down the basic difference between sensitive skin serums and other serums, our contributor Dr. Zeeshan Afzal, MD — Welzo, an online healthcare platform says, “Sensitive skin serums are formulated to be extra gentle on the skin and to not irritate delicate skin, typically avoiding ingredients that can cause reactions, unlike other serums which may contain more active or potent ingredients.”

According to a board-certified dermatologist, Andrea Suarez, MD, FAAD, “Sensitive skin refers to unpleasant sensory experiences when your skin comes in contact with things that are otherwise innocuous”. In some cases, lack of skin care products can even lead to cancer if you expose yourself too much to harsh UV lights. That is why dermatologists always recommend using skin care products.

Considering the wide range of products in the market, you can’t blame yourself if you were deceived by claims on a product’s packaging or in an advertisement. It’s just impossible to find the right one on your own, so our team of experts have curated 15 best serums for sensitive skins from across the internet so that you don’t have to break a sweat.

    How to Choose the Best Serum for Sensitive Skin?

    You need to consider these few things when choosing the best serum for you.

    Skin Type: This is the most important factor that you need to consider. If you have oily, dull, acne-prone, or dry skin, you need to choose serums with the right type of ingredients so that they don’t cause any unexpected reactions. If you have oily skin, you will need to choose a product that provides hydration and has non-comedogenic properties. If you have dry skin, then you are going to need a vitamin C serum for sensitive skin.

    Skin Issue: You will have to identify the type of issue you are experiencing. For example, if you have started seeing aging signs, maybe something like Eclat Natural Skincare Vitamin C Serum will work the best. And, if you are proactively trying to prevent any type of skin issue, you can choose something like the all-in-one Vibriance Super C Penetrating Serum.

    Ingredients: Vitamin C is a common ingredient on most of these products, but there are other ingredients that you need to look out for. When you have sensitive skin, you are prone to certain ingredients which can cause severe allergic reactions. So, carefully read all the ingredients and ensure that you are not allergic to any of them.

    According to Dr. B.R.N. Padmini, here are some of the safest ingredients for sensitive skin:

    • “Hyaluronic Acid: Hyaluronic acid is a hydrating ingredient that helps retain moisture in the skin without causing irritation. It's suitable for all skin types, including sensitive skin.
    • Glycerin: Glycerin is a humectant that attracts and retains moisture in the skin, providing hydration without causing irritation.
    • Aloe Vera: Aloe vera is known for its soothing and calming properties. It can help reduce redness and irritation in sensitive skin.
    • Chamomile: Chamomile has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties, making it beneficial for calming sensitive skin and reducing redness.
    • Green Tea Extract: Green tea is rich in antioxidants and has anti-inflammatory properties, making it suitable for sensitive skin and helping to reduce irritation.
    • Colloidal Oatmeal: Colloidal oatmeal is finely ground oatmeal suspended in liquid and is known for its soothing and moisturizing properties. It can help relieve itchiness and irritation in sensitive skin.
    • Shea Butter: Shea butter is a natural moisturizer that helps hydrate and soothe the skin, making it a good option for sensitive skin.
    • Ceramides: Ceramides help strengthen the skin's barrier and retain moisture, making them beneficial for sensitive skin prone to dryness.

    Your skin care professional either dermatologist or plastic surgeon, may suggest to you the right product which is appropriate for you based  on your specific skin  type, so that the chances of allergy, pigmentation are minimized.”
