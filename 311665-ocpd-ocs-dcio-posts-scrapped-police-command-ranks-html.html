OCPD, OCS and DCIO posts scrapped in new police command ranks

February 02, 2024
blog

- Leadership in the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) has also been affected

- Several police units have been merged into new units under a different command system

- NPS will deploy Ward Police Commanders in every ward who will be in charge of police operations

- They will be expected to implement NPS policy of making police stations to be centre of policing services

Ongoing re-organisation of the National Police Service has rid-off most popular titles that have been synonymous with the police command including OCPDs, OCSs and DCIOs.

The restructuring of the NPS has altered Kenya Police and Administration Police services as well as Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) leadership.

The reforms have led to end of former divisional police commanders popularly known as OCPDs, Divisional Criminal Investigations bosses known as DCIOs and District Administration Police commanders (DAPCs).

The OCPDs and DCIOs have been replaced with sub county police and DCI commanders while the DAPCs have been scrapped.

Also scrapped are base commanders and the Divisional Traffic Officers popularly known as DTOs who were in charge of traffic police in stations and divisions.

The traffic police department was renamed to the Traffic Police Unit within KPS which will be under the sub county commanders.

The NPS board toughened rules for officers in traffic duties through a directive that requires a police officer to have served for at least five years before they can be posted to the new TPU.

All the other police officers in every jurisdiction will be under command of the sub county police commander.

Inspector General Hillary Mutyambai on July 12 announced that the NPS will deploy Ward Police Commanders in every ward who will be in charge of police operations.

These will replace the police station bosses -the Officers Commanding Stations – popularly known as OCSs.

The OCSs will be renamed Ward Police Commanders and tasked with police leadership and command in a ward.

“The next move is identification and deployment of suitable ward police commanders famously known as OCSs,” Mutyambai said.

The IG said the new team of WPC will be expected to implement the NPS policy of making police stations to be the centre of policing services in the NPS.

"It is at the stations where the rubber meets the road. If things are right at the police station, most of the objectives of the NPS will be accomplished," he said.

The police boss said as part of police reforms, they will digitize the occurrence book and other police records. Also they will develop a case file management system so as to upgrade operations and investigations.

In the APS, there will only be the Sub County commander in charge of the Critical Infrastructure Protection Unit (CIPU). The officers will deployed from their camps and will not undertake any other duties.

The unit is mandated with protection of vital installations including SGR, wind energy stations, geothermal development and oil exploration sites, and state dams among others. They will be only deployed in sub counties where there are such projects.

Traffic base commanders have also been scrapped after the Traffic Department was merged with KPS as a unit – Traffic Police Unit and placed under the regular police command.

Chiefs, assistant county commissioners will no longer have the AP officers to them. They will be required to liaise with WPC whenever they need assistance to execute their duties.

The DCI has not been left out in the changes. The Divisional Criminal Investigations DCIOs known as DCI’s Flying Squad lost its independence from the respective county DCI commands after they were placed under County DCI chiefs.

Other operational units including Special Crimes Prevention Unit, Anti-Terrorism Police Unit Anti-and Bomb Disposal Unit retained their independence.

In the changes, APS’s Stock Theft Prevention Unit (APSTPU) and KPS’s Anti Stock Theft Unit (ASTU) which have been doing the same work under different command have been merged into ASTU which was moved to APS.

Other than the General Service Unit the TPU, Kenya Police Dog Unit, Diplomatic Police Unit (DPU) Kenya Airports Police Unit (KAPU) and Kenya Railways Police Unit (KRPU) are some of the units that will be under the KPS.

Others include Maritime Police Unit, National Disaster Management Unit, Presidential Escort Unit, Kenya Tourists Police Unit and the troubled Kenya Police Air Wing which will be renamed the National Police Air-wing.

The GSU officers in various camps countrywide will be under the respective commands except those in specialised units

The next phase of changes will be in the NPS training colleges. The AP training College previously renamed to the National Police Service Training College, Embakasi “A” campus will only offer basic training for officers who will only serve in the forms of units under the AP.

The units include ASTU, Border Police Unit, Rapid Deployment Unit and the Security of Government Buildings Unit and CIPU.

Senior APS officers will be trained at the APS’s senior college in Emali, Makueni county.

The GSU training college has been renamed the NPS College Embakasi ‘B’ Campus and will remain the basic training base for GSU officers.

After the changes are completed, police officers in general duties will not be allowed to wear jungle uniforms. They will only don the light blue uniforms.

The APS will retain the Border School as the centre for specialized training for BPU and the unit’s headquarter while KPS will have the Seniors Training College in Loresho as the leadership development centre.
