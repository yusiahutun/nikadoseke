Jason Aldean, Lights Come On [Listen]

April 26, 2024
blog

Jason Aldean returns to a sound his longtime fans are familiar with on “Lights Come On,” the first single from his next studio album. There’s not a whiff of R&B influence to be found on this straightforward country rocker. It’s a song built for his live show.

Lyrically, "Lights Come On" is about that needed weekend release after a long 40 hours at the job. For the first time (and quite uniquely) he turns the camera on himself — especially at the bridge — but the song plays only to his crowd. After a few years away from his blue collar roots, Aldean returns to a sound he popularized with songs like “My Kinda Party” and “She’s Country.” It's a straightforward, no-thinking-needed rock song that relies on scorching electric guitars and an energy we all can't wait to feel once again.

For Aldean and many of today’s top male country stars, it’s all about the live show, so many singles (especially first singles from new albums) are built to feed that high-powered machine first. Radio’s needs have never run counter to this philosophy — an artist is tacitly encouraged to return to what’s worked before. “Lights Come On” does that and is certain to be a hit with fans in cars, at work, or in front of his electrifying stage on a Friday or Saturday night.

Did You Know?: Aldean is busy working on his seventh studio album. The previously three have all been released in October or November.
