Rosalind Ross Career

Back in 2004, Rosalind won a bronze medal while being part of the USA’s CoastLine Team at the World Vaulting Championship. The following year, she became part of Mount Eden Vaulting Club, later to which she became a member of the team FAME and with the help of which she brought home a first-ever gold medal for her appearance at the competition of Alltech FEI World Equestrian Games. Thereafter, she won the gold medal again for the FAME in the year 2008 and 2010 while participating in the World Equestrian Games. Although she marked a great start to her career in athletics, she decided to switch her career from being a Vaulting Championship to a screenwriter.

She got her first project in 2014 while working as a screenwriter and got it from the Amazon Prime show “Matador”. That same year, Ross started getting various projects for screenwriting including the short film Homeward. Later in 2016, she got another project for a short film called “A Social Life”. She has also worked on another project, Lioness. Besides writing, Ross describes herself as an American beauty who has taken the work of being a mentor at various charity houses including AMBITION.
