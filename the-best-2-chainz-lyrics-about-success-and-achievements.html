The best 2 Chainz lyrics about Success and Achievements

March 12, 2024
blog

The theme of success and achievements is one that is prevalent in various forms of expression, including music. Hip-hop, in particular, often emphasizes the pursuit and celebration of success. One artist who exemplifies this theme in his lyrics is 2 Chainz. Through his music, 2 Chainz delves into the world of aspirations, accomplishments, and the lavish lifestyle that often accompanies success. Let's explore this theme through the lyrics of 2 Chainz, examining four to six specific examples that showcase his preoccupation with success.In his song "I'm Different," off his 2012 album "Based on a T.R.U. Story," 2 Chainz declares his distinctive nature and highlights his accomplishments. He raps, "I'm different, yeah, I'm different / I'm different, yeah, I'm different / I'm different, yeah, I'm different / Pull up to the scene with my ceiling missing." These lines exemplify his confidence and success, as he expresses his uniqueness and shows off the luxurious lifestyle he has achieved, symbolized by a car with a missing roof.Another example of 2 Chainz's emphasis on success and achievements can be found in his song "No Lie," featuring Drake, also from his debut album. He raps, "Tell 'em give me $50 million or I'ma quit," suggesting his relentless pursuit of financial success. This line showcases his drive and ambition, as he sets a seemingly impossible goal, solidifying his determination to achieve extraordinary levels of wealth.In the song "Birthday Song" from his album "Based on a T.R.U. Story," 2 Chainz provides a more playful representation of his success. He boasts, "It's your birthday, it's your birthday / Bad bitch contest, you're in first place / You in first place, you in first place / Bad bitch contest, you in first place." Through these lines, he brings attention to his ability to attract and be surrounded by beautiful women, showcasing the rewards that success can bring.One can also find evidence of this theme in 2 Chainz's collaboration with Lil Wayne on the track "Bounce," from his 2013 album "B.O.A.T.S. II: Me Time." The artists rap, "I started with a dollar and a dream that dollar turn into millions / I gave my mama a milli and my watch just a couple of hundred." This particular verse reflects on their humble beginnings and the milestones they have achieved. They emphasize the substantial monetary success they have attained, as well as their generosity towards their loved ones.Furthermore, in the track "Watch Out" from his 2015 mixtape "Trap-A-Velli Tre," 2 Chainz celebrates overcoming obstacles and achieving success. He raps, "She used to spend that gas money on the bills / Now she falling every time I'm walking in the hills." Here, he illustrates the narrative of someone who has gone from financial struggle to being able to afford a luxurious lifestyle. This song serves as a reminder for listeners that success can be attainable with hard work and perseverance.In conclusion, 2 Chainz consistently touches upon the theme of success and achievements throughout his music. Through his lyrics, he hones in on the pursuit of success and the rewards that come with it. Whether he's celebrating his unique nature, setting ambitious financial goals, or reflecting on his journey from humble beginnings to incredible heights, 2 Chainz's lyrics encapsulate the essence of the success and achievements theme. With his confident and boastful delivery, his music serves as an anthem for those who aspire to reach their own levels of accomplishment and enjoy the fruits of their labor.
