The 13 most on-trend sofa colours of 2024 is yours up there?

March 29, 2024
blog
1

Grey

Darlings of Chelsea

Unsurprisingly, the perennially popular grey was the most sought-after sofa colour of the year. Infinitely versatile, a neutral grey can be adapted for almost any home, with styles running the gamut from a traditional textured tweed to a modern tactile velvet. "Grey goes brilliantly with a neutral colour palette to give the perfect temperature balance between those cool tones from the grey and the warm tones from the neutrals," says Yvonne Keal, senior product manager at Hillarys.

Pictured: Holland Grand Chaise Sofa in Linen Cotton Oyster

2

Green

Dunelm

"I think the rise of green interiors is to do with more people wanting to bring the outside into their homes," says Ben. Whilst crisp apple green shades sit comfortably in a country cottage setting, forest green creates a moodier and more romantic feel.

Pictured: Martha Vintage Velvet Corner Chaise at Dunelm

3

Cream

Dunelm

Classic cream was not as highly sought-after as one might expect, despite being the picture of versatility. A brilliant neutral base from which to build, with warm undertones that invite richly patterned textiles, colourful accessories, and reddish woods. Maintain a cooler colour scheme by pairing your sofa with greys or soft blues.

Pictured: Skye Slub Faux Linen Snuggle Chair at Dunelm

4

Blue

Mark Scott

Another versatile choice, dark blue velvet sofas are luxurious, whilst pillowy powder blue sofas are a bit more country cottage cosy. "Blue is a calming and tranquil colour, perfect for deep thought and introspection," says Ben. "Since a lot of us now work from home more often, this makes it a great colour scheme for concentration.”

Pictured: Country Living Witcombe Sofa at DFS

5

Pink

Rachel Whiting

A surprising entry – pink sofas are in fact more popular than beige – but a welcome injection of colour into our homes. Decorating with pink requires a keen eye to avoid an overly saccharine outcome; a chalky pink or a rosy pink/red can be really sophisticated especially when used on heavily textured fabrics like wool, velvet or tweed.

Pictured: Country Living Kirkton Sofa at DFS

6

Black

Heal's

Somewhat surprisingly, black made it to sixth place. Bold and moody in expression, if a little tricky to pull off, black sofas work best when you fully embrace the theme – paired with dark wood floors, antiqued brass, and soft furnishings in romantic shades of grape or burgundy.

Pictured: Eton Sofa at Heal's

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

7

Beige

Marks & Spencer

Classic beige came in seventh place – in the battle of the neutrals, beige sofas are less popular than cooler creams, but more popular than white. Beige lends itself to warm living room schemes like this one, and can be layered to great effect with close shades like biscuit and brown.

Pictured: Erin Sofa at Marks & Spencer

8

Orange

Lonf

A joyful choice making it into the top 10 most popular sofa colours for 2024. A spicy and warm orange like this Pudding sofa from Loaf brightens up a room, and is a deceptively versatile centrepiece, sitting comfortably with natural materials and other warming accents such as brass.

Pictured: Pudding Sofa Bed at Loaf

9

Teal

Dan Duchars | Sofology

The third blue shade to make it onto this year's list. Greener variations like a teal or sapphire introduce rich undertones that sit well when contrasted with chalkier pastels. "Eye-catching, versatile and practical, a teal, navy or deep blue sofa could make the perfect addition to your living room and are a great way to make your room pop," says Ben.

Pictured: Sofology X George Clarke Midland Hill Sofa at Sofology

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

White

DFS

A much-maligned shade for pet owners and parents. A pristine white sofa can look like it has been lifted from the pages of a glossy magazine, and invites a minimalist design scheme with a sophisticated yet sparing use of interesting accessories. Warmer shades are a more forgiving choice, and offer a neutral base for layers of pastel cushions and throws.

Pictured: Hugh Sofa at DFS

11

Navy

John Lewis & Partners

A rich navy is the moodier counterpart to the classic blue option above. "To create drama in the space, go for a deeper, rich blue," says Kelly Collins, interior designer and head of creative at Swyft Home. "One of the best things about blue is that it works with a large number of different colours and its broad range of tones can also change the look and feel of any space."

Pictured: John Lewis Otley Large 3 Seater Sofa

12

Brown

Soho Home

A brown sofa doesn't necessarily mean leather, although a rich tan leather sofa is a failsafe option. We love the caramel linen on this Soho Home sofa. A soft brown sofa like this really calls for laidback styling and a calming colour palette.

Pictured: Audrey Sofa at Soho Home

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

13

Yellow

John Lewis & Partners

Yellow is deceptively versatile, sitting quite happily with most colours on the colour wheel – although most effective with softer and nature-inspired greens and blues. We expect cheery yellows to grow in popularity in 2023 and beyond.

Pictured: Camber Sofa at John Lewis
